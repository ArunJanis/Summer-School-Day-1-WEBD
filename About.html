About ME


INTRODUCTION

My name is Arun Janakiraman, and I am currently pursuing a degree in Aerospace Engineering. I am deeply passionate about designing, innovating, and solving real-world problems using technology. My interests span across aerodynamics, propulsion systems, and sustainable engineering solutions.

Academic Background

I am in my early semesters of aerospace engineering and have already been involved in several academic projects, including designing a 600 SHP turboshaft engine and participating in simulations using ANSYS and SolidWorks. I also actively engage in coding, particularly learning C programming and web development to expand my technical skill set.

Projects and Achievements

Some of my notable projects include designing a seabed cleaner to tackle ocean microplastic pollution, and contributing to drone and RC plane design competitions. I've also interned with organizations like Frynman Aerospace, where I worked on airfoil optimization and aerodynamic modeling.

Current Focus

At present, I am learning full-stack web development, starting with HTML and exploring backend technologies soon. I enjoy combining my engineering knowledge with modern software tools to create practical and innovative solutions.

Personal Traits

I am a quick learner, self-motivated, and always eager to dive deep into any subject I take on. I believe in giving my full effort to every task and take pride in being dependable and consistent in my work.

Conclusion

I aspire to build a career that bridges aerospace engineering and sustainable innovation. With a passion for both mechanical systems and digital tools, I aim to contribute meaningfully to the future of technology and the environment.
